Provolone Build Log

May 14, 2017

Provolone is the initial robot designed by the official ODU VEX U competition team for the 2017-2018 world robotics competition "VEX U: In the Zone" (Resources, VEX U Info).

Our goals for the design of Provolone are to meet three criteria:

  1. Simple
  2. Robust
  3. Effective
Generally speaking, we plan to avoid any complicated or untested components as we continue to develop this competition robot design.

Currently we have a base subsystem designed and constructed, that is capable of movement. The base design currently uses four 4" omni-wheels in an H-drive pattern, directly connected to "High Speed" geared 393 motors.





Design Motifs

  1. Modular motor plates: To reduce the time and effort needed to replace and diagnose drive motors, we use a modular 3x5 plate. Taking off a motor then requires removing six easily accessible hex screws.

  2. Diagonal drive template: The bot is designed to meet the size constraints only when diagonally placed in the sizing box. This allows us to A) Employ self-directed size/weight limits, and B) Allow more overall length for the lift bar system, and claw size.

  3. Jam-nut standoffs: In an attempt to improve tower stability, we implemented four jam-nut standoffs, constructed from 2" standoffs and a pair of Keps nuts. These are placed near the bottom of the base.

  4. Triangular strut: To improve tower stability, we implemented a triangular strut out of standoffs, 45-degree angle gussets, and three C-channels. Our aim is to make a structurally sound lift tower without using up a lot of space and weight.
